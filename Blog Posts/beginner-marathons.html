The 13 Best Marathons for Beginners: Your Guide to a Memorable First Marathon

Introduction

Starting your first marathon journey is an exhilarating and life-changing experience. As a beginner, selecting the right marathon can significantly impact your overall enjoyment and achievements in the race.

With an abundance of marathons to choose from, the process of selecting one can be both exciting and overwhelming. It's easy to get caught up in exploring the options, reading about each race's unique offerings. However, the sheer number of choices can lead to decision paralysis, causing us to procrastinate and never actually commit to a marathon.

I've personally experienced this dilemma countless times, finding myself opening numerous tabs on my computer with different marathon websites, leaving them open for days, only to eventually close them all without making a decision. To help alleviate this challenge, I've compiled a curated list of popular marathons that are particularly well-suited for first-time participants.

What you need to consider in your decision making

  • Timing and Weather:Evaluate the date and time of the marathon. Research the weather conditions during that period, both at the marathon location and in your hometown. Understand how the climate may affect your training leading up to the event. For instance, if you opt for a spring marathon, prepare for potentially challenging winter training conditions. Likewise, for a fall marathon, consider the heat you might encounter during summer training.

  • Course Characteristics:Analyze the marathon route to determine its terrain and surface. Find out if it is predominantly on paved roads or if it incorporates other surfaces like trails. Ascertain whether the course is mostly flat or if it presents significant elevation changes and challenging hills. Choosing a course that aligns with your preferences and strengths can enhance your overall race experience.

  • Location and Travel:Consider the location of the marathon and assess how far you are willing to travel. Factor in the climate of the marathon destination compared to your hometown. Determine if you need to adapt to a different climate and allow extra time for acclimatization. Additionally, evaluate the logistical aspects of traveling to the marathon, such as transportation, accommodation, and associated costs.

  • Personal Goals and Preferences:Reflect on your personal goals for the marathon. Are you seeking a fast and flat course to target a personal record, or do you prefer a more scenic and challenging route? Consider your preferences for race atmosphere, crowd support, and potential participation in a larger or smaller event. Aligning these factors with your aspirations can contribute to a more fulfilling marathon experience.

  • Reputation and Reviews:Research the marathon's reputation and read reviews from previous participants. Look for feedback on the event organization, course markings, aid stations, crowd support, and overall runner experience. Learning from others' experiences can provide valuable insights to help inform your decision.

How long does it take to train for a marathon?

While the specific duration can vary depending on individual factors and training plans, there are some general guidelines to consider.

The recommended timeframe for marathon training is typically around 16 to 20 weeks. This duration allows runners to gradually build their endurance, improve their fitness level, and prepare their bodies for the demanding 26.2-mile race.
However, it's important to note that the duration can be adjusted based on an individual's current fitness level and running experience.

For beginners who have little to no previous running experience, it is advisable to allow for a longer training period to establish a solid foundation of running fitness and gradually increase mileage without risking injury or overexertion. A training plan of at least 24 weeks would be more suitable for beginners to comfortably progress and adapt to the demands of marathon training.

On the other hand, experienced runners who have a solid running base and prior long-distance race experience may be able to follow a shorter training schedule, ranging from 12 to 16 weeks.

It's important to remember that marathon training is not just about logging miles. A training program should include a combination of long runs, speed workouts, tempo runs, cross-training, strength training, and rest days. These elements help prevent injury, improve performance, and enhance overall fitness.

Keep in mind that every individual is unique, and training requirements may vary. Factors such as age, fitness level, previous injuries, and personal goals should be taken into consideration when determining the appropriate duration of marathon training. Consulting with a running coach or experienced marathoner can provide valuable insights and guidance tailored to your specific needs.

  1. Napa Valley Marathon

    Experience the breathtaking beauty of California's Napa Valley at the Napa Valley Marathon, a picturesque and scenic race through renowned wine country. This marathon offers a point-to-point course along the enchanting Silverado Trail, allowing you to soak in the lush green hills, stunning vineyards, and world-class wineries.
    With its supportive atmosphere, relaxed vibe and reputation for taking care of runners, it's a fantastic choice for first-time marathoners.
    The Napa Valley Marathon combines stunning vistas, a relaxed ambiance, and a chance to immerse yourself in the vibrant wine culture of the region. Don't miss out on this unforgettable experience that blends athleticism and natural splendor.

    https://napavalleymarathon.org/

  2. Missoula Marathon

    The Missoula Marathon in Montana offers a scenic course through the streets of Missoula, along the Clark Fork River, and across stunning countryside.
    Known for its friendly volunteers and supportive community, it's perfect for first time marathoners. It has even been recognized multiple times as the best marathon in the United States.
    The mostly flat, point-to-point route is ideal for those chasing faster times, with a challenging halfway hill offering rewarding views.
    Experience the majesty of Missoula as you join 1,750 runners in a memorable race through valleys and mountains. The downtown finish awaits with enthusiastic cheers and a unique horseshoe-shaped medal. Don't miss this remarkable event.

    https://www.missoulamarathon.org/

  3. Grandma's Marathon

    Grandma's Marathon is ideal for beginners due to its beginner-friendly features. With a relatively flat course, including some gentle rolling hills and a larger incline before mile 22, it offers a manageable terrain for first-time marathoners.
    The race takes place in June in Duluth, Minnesota, ensuring cooler weather that is conducive to running. Additionally, the event has gained a reputation for its well-organized nature and supportive atmosphere, with loud crowds of spectators providing encouragement along the way.
    The combination of small-town charm, scenic views of Lake Superior, and enthusiastic community support make Grandma's Marathon a perfect starting point for beginners embarking on their marathon journey.

    https://grandmasmarathon.com/

  4. The Bank of America Chicago Marathon

    The Bank of America Chicago Marathon is one of the easiest marathons in the US and is therefore, an ideal choice for first-time marathoners seeking a fast and flat course.
    Held in Chicago, Illinois, this iconic marathon takes participants through the vibrant streets and diverse neighborhoods of the city. With its reputation for producing personal best times and world records, the Chicago Marathon offers an exciting and supportive atmosphere for runners of all levels. The race boasts a massive crowd of 1.7 million spectators, ensuring ample encouragement throughout the course.
    Whether you're drawn to the party atmosphere or the scenic tour of Chicago's landmarks, the Chicago Marathon is a must-try for beginners and experienced runners alike.
